Implantable Automatic Defibrillators: Since 1980

Image representing heart protection by an ICD

Presentation of Implantable Automatic Defibrillators (ICDs)

Implantable automatic defibrillators (ICDs) are essential for managing severe cardiac arrhythmias. By continuously monitoring heart rhythm and delivering electric shocks in emergencies, these devices save lives and improve patients' quality of life. This timeline illustrates the technological advancements and impact of ICDs on cardiovascular health.

1960 - Invention of the external defibrillator
American cardiologist Paul Zoll was the first to develop an external defibrillator to treat cardiac arrhythmias via electrodes placed on the chest.
1970 - First implantable defibrillator theorized
American cardiologist Paul Zoll was the first to develop an implantable defibrillator to treat cardiac arrhythmias.
1980 - First ICD implanted in a human
Michel Mirowski, along with Morton Mower, implanted the first ICD in a patient.
1990 - Improvement of ICD technologies
ICDs became smaller and more efficient.
2000 - ICD with resynchronization therapy
Introduction of ICDs combined with cardiac resynchronization devices.
2010 - Wireless and self-implantable ICDs
Development of wireless and miniaturized intracardiac ICDs.
2020 - ICD with remote monitoring capabilities
ICDs integrate telemedicine capabilities.
2050 - Nanotechnological ICD
Miniaturized at the microscopic scale using nanotechnology.
